Command Structure

Every order has a lane.

A clean chain keeps the cartel profitable. Members know who makes calls, who handles pressure, and who keeps the street quiet.

Owner

Final authority over money, alliances, wars, and discipline.

  • Approves major operations
  • Controls high value negotiations
  • Settles betrayal and debt matters

Intelligence

Information control, surveillance, and trusted reporting for delicate moves.

  • Tracks rivals and opportunities
  • Builds leverage files
  • Briefs command before operations

Enforcer

Masked muscle used for pressure, recovery, and protection.

  • Secures meeting locations
  • Prevents ambushes
  • Handles quiet punishment

Quartermaster

Controls supplies, equipment, vehicles, and route readiness.

  • Manages inventory
  • Prepares convoy resources
  • Keeps crews properly equipped

Supervisor

Oversees crews, keeps standards tight, and reports problems upward.

  • Assigns daily work
  • Checks discipline and comms
  • Coordinates with command

Lieutenants

Trusted crew leaders who keep operations moving without noise.

  • Lead route teams
  • Manage small crews
  • Handle local decisions

Sicarios

Specialized pressure unit for protection, retaliation, and recovery.

  • Move with discipline
  • Guard high value meetings
  • Act only when ordered

Operations

Planning team focused on routes, timing, ambush prevention, and execution.

  • Build operation plans
  • Assign staging points
  • Track outcomes and losses

Smugglers

Movement specialists trusted with product, cash, messages, and controlled drops.

  • Run low profile routes
  • Maintain clean handoffs
  • Report route risks

Associates

Entry members proving loyalty, patience, and discipline inside the organization.

  • Support assigned crews
  • Observe meetings
  • Learn cartel standards

Scouts

Lookouts and early warning support for convoys, meetings, and street movement.

  • Watch entrances and exits
  • Call out movement
  • Keep the crew informed
Promotion Track

Earned through silence and results.

Step One

Show up, listen, and follow comms without creating heat.

Step Two

Complete assigned work and protect the crew during simple operations.

Step Three

Take responsibility for routes, meetings, or enforcement details.

Step Four

Lead profitable work with no wasted conflict and no loose ends.
